Managing Permissions

Access to documents and folders

What are they?

Permissions are rules that determine what a user can see and do with information in GCDOCS. They determine who can view, edit, and delete items.

These permissions—or types of access—can range from only being able to see the document title, opening a read-only copy, editing it, or deleting it.

Managing Permissions

Permissions are usually pre-set when you first get access to GCDOCS, based on your business unit’s information management procedures.

Typically, the owner of the document (the one who saves it into GCDOCS), can manage its Permissions. If the owner wants to give others a certain type of access to the document, then the owner updates or modifies the existing Permissions. This may be useful, for example, if one wants to collaborate on the writing or editing of a document.

The 'updating' of permissions can be done either at the document level, by giving access to a specific document, or at the folder level, giving access to all the documents within a selected folder.

The types of access you can give can range from:

  • See: users can see the document title but not open it.
  • See Contents: users can open a read-only version.
  • Modify: allows users to rename, and edit the description of a document.
  • Edit Attributes: users can modify the categories.
  • Reserve: allows users to reserve a document and save new versions.
  • Delete Versions: users can delete previous versions.
  • Delete: users can delete the document.
  • Edit Permissions: users can modify the document's permissions.
