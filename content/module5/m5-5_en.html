Key concepts and skills

In this module you learned about the different ways you can save and organize information within GCDOCS. Here's some key concepts to remember:

Collections

  • Collections allows users to group information together, making it easy to share and collaborate with colleagues. Unlike a folder, where the documents reside within the container, a Collection contains links to the documents.
  • Collections can be stored in one's Personal Workspace for personal use or in the Enterprise workspace to share with colleagues.

Adding documents with Drag and Drop

  • Drag and Drop is one way of adding new and existing documents from your desktop or shared drive to GCDOCS.
  • To review all the steps, select:

How to drag and drop

Adding documents using Enterprise Connect

  • Enterprise Connect allows you to save a document directly from your desktop. Enterprise Connect integrates GCDOCS with applications like MS Word, PowerPoint, and Excel.
  • Saving documents to GCDOCS right from your desktop applications is quick and easy.

How to save a document using Enterprise Connect

Adding email using Enterprise Connect

  • Email can also be added to GCDOCS using Enterprise Connect.
  • Saving emails that capture business information ensures that important business decisions and transactions with clients are appropriately managed and accessible to others.
  • It's good practice to wait until an email thread is complete (with all relevant forwards/replies) before saving to GCDOCS.
  • To review the procedure, select:

How to add an email using Enterprise Connect

Managing Permissions

  • Permissions are rules that determine what users can see and do with information in GCDOCS. They determine who can view, edit, and delete items.
  • Permissions range from only being able to see the document title, opening a read-only copy, editing it, or deleting it.
  • Typically, the owner of the document manages its Permissions. The
    owner can usually 'update' or modify existing Permissions to give others a certain type of access.
  • Permissions can be managed at either the document or folder level.
  • The Function menu next to the document, or folder, gives access to the Permissions feature.
